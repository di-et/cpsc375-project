### World Bank Series Selected
The following series was downloaded from the World Bank DataBank and extracted data looking at the following series name's from the years 2022 and 2023:
- Population ages 80 and above, female (SP.POP.80UP.FE)
- Population ages 80 and above, male (SP.POP.80UP.MA)
- Population, total (SP.POP.TOTL)
- Life expectancy at birth, total (SP.DYN.LE00.IN)
- Death rate, crude per 1,000 (SP.DYN.CDRT.IN)
- Population ages 0-14, female/male (SP.POP.0014.FE.IN / MA.IN)
- Population ages 15-64, female/male (SP.POP.1564.FE.IN / MA.IN)
- Population ages 65 and above, female/male (SP.POP.65UP.FE.IN / MA.IN)

These series were selected because COVID-19 disproportionately affected elderly populations compared to younger age groups. This made age group the essiential variable for predicting death rates. Additionally, life expectancy and crude death rate were also indicators for overall healthcare quality across countries.
